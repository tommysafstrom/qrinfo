Sorbus ulleungensis 'Dodong' E

Common name: ullungrönn 'Dodong' E, Ulleung rowan 'Dodong'
Family: Rosaceae (Rose family)
Synonym: Sorbus ulleungensis 'Dodong'
Distribution: East Asia: South Korea (Ulleung Island)
Hardiness: Zone 4, Swedish hardiness zone map for woody plants
Life form: Deciduous tree
Usage: Trees
Looks like: Sharper leaves than Sorbus decora.
Size: H: 9-12 m, W: 4-5 m
Cultivation: Sunny, well-drained moisture-retaining soil in a position that does not get too hot. Like other rowans, it is not suitable as a street tree.
Pests: Very popular with hares and may need protection.
Horticultural merit: E-planta
Description: Medium-sized tree with open, long, upturned, powerful branches.
It comes into leaf early. Leaves turn large, shiny fresh green. Sharper leaves than Sorbus decora. Perceived as exotic. Yellow-white flowers in large bunches. Shiny orange-red berries. Strong autumn colours in yellow/orange/red.
