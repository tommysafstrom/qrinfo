Buddleja davidii 'Ile de France'

Common name: syrenbuddleja 'Ile de France', butterfly bush 'île de France'
Family: Scrophulariaceae (figwort family)
Distribution: East Asia: China, Japan
Habitat: Thickets on mountain slopes, forest clearings and rocky stream banks
Hardiness: Zone 3, Swedish hardiness zone map for woody plants
Life form: Deciduous shrub/sub-shrub
Usage: Shrubs, Large Shrubs/Tree-like shrubs
Stress & Pronounciation: Buddleja dāvīdiī 'Ile de France'
Classical pronunciation: /budː'lejːa daː'wiːdiiː il də fʁɑ̃s/
Ecclesiastical pronunciation: /budː'lejːa da'vidii il də fʁɑ̃s/
(Pronounciation key)
Design: This shrub is great for flower borders, wall-side borders and as a foundation in plantings. Great for butterflies
Size: Height/width up to 2,5 m (in Sweden)
Cultivation: Sun - partial shade in well-drained soil.
Description: Arching branches with lance-shaped leaves. Long racemes of deep purple flowers with yellow-orange spots in the middle that are present from summer to autumn.
