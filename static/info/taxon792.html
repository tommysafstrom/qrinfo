Neillia incisa 'Crispa'

Common name: liten stefanandra, lace shrub 'Crispa'
Family: Rosaceae (Rose family)
Synonym: Stephanandra incisa 'Crispa'
Distribution: E Asia: China, Japan, Korea & Taiwan
Habitat: The species is found on mountain slopes at 500-1000 m above sea level, often near streams.
Hardiness: Zone 4, Swedish hardiness zone map for woody plants
Life form: Deciduous shrub/sub-shrub
Usage: Shrubs
Growth habit - form: Low-growing woody ground cover with arching branches.
Design: Hanging beautifully over walls and edges.
Name - meaning & etymology: The family name 'Neillia' honours the Scottish horticulturalist and naturalist Patrick Neill (1776-1851), one of the founders of the Wernerian Natural History Society and the Caledonian Horticultural Society.
The species epithet 'incisa' refers to the deeply and sharply incised leaves, from latin 'incīsus' - cut, carved.
