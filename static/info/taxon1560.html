Hydrangea paniculata 'Grandiflora'

Common name: syrenhortensia, hardy white-flowered hydrangea
Family: Hydrangeaceae (Hydrangea family)
Distribution: Eastern Asia: China, Korea, Japan, Russia
Habitat: Forests and thickets in valleys or mountain slopes
Hardiness: Zone 4, Swedish hardiness zone map for woody plants
Life form: Deciduous shrub/sub-shrub
Usage: Large Shrubs/Tree-like shrubs
Size: H: 2-4 m; W: 1-2.5 m
Design: For gardens and parks.
Cultivation: Sun-partial shade in well-drained, moisture-retaining neutral to acidic soil.
Description: Larger shrub trees with vase-shaped growth. Blooms for a long time with large white-white-yellow, later pink, mainly sterile flower clusters.
