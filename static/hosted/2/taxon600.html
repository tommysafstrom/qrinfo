Taxon

Quercus palustris

Common name: kärrek, pin oak
Family: Fagaceae (Beech family)
Distribution: C & E North America
Habitat: Alluvial floodplains in soils with a high clay content
IUCN Red list: Least concern
Hardiness: Zone 3, Swedish hardiness zone map for woody plants
Life form: Deciduous tree
Usage: Large trees
Horticultural merit: RHS Award of Garden Merit (AGM)
Size: H: 18-22 m (10-15 m in Sv.); W: 8-14 m
Design: Light shade, good for use as new tree plantings in the urban environment. Resistant to air pollution. Nice autumn color. Considered the most easily established oak species. Does not require mashy conditions
Leaf: About 15 cm, leathery, ovate and deeply lobed with a sawn edge. Beautiful autumn colors.
Cultivation: Sun to partial shade in moist, well-drained acidic to neutral soil.
Description: Medium to large tree with broad cone-shaped airy crown. Continuous trunk, with many and weak branches. Horizontal branches of young trees, but the lower ones bend downwards with increasing age. The upper lateral branches begin to grow more vigorously and become more upward, and the crown shape becomes more irregular and broadly ovate (occurs after 40-50 years).
