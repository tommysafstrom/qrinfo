Hydrangea arborescens 'Annabelle'

Common name: vidjehortensia 'Annabelle', hydrangea 'Annabelle'
Family: Hydrangeaceae (Hydrangea family)
Distribution: Eastern North America
Habitat: Under forest canopy and along streams in moist soils
Hardiness: Zone 4, Swedish hardiness zone map for woody plants
Life form: Deciduous shrub/sub-shrub
Usage: Shrubs
Size: H: 1.2-1.6 m; W: 1.5 m
Cultivation: Sun-partial shade in well-drained, moisture-retaining neutral to acidic soil. Cut down hard in learly spring.
Horticultural merit: RHS Award of Garden Merit (AGM)
Description: Large-leaved shrub with large flower clusters in late summer.
