Fritillaria imperialis 'Lutea'

Common name: sort av kejsarkrona, Crown imperial 'Lutea'
Family: Liliaceae
Hardiness: RHS H7, <-20 °C, very hardy
Life form: Geophyte (bulb, corm or tuber)
Usage: Bulbs
Flower bulb: The Crown imperial is a beloved spring flowering plant that has been used in Europe for almost 450 years. It is robust, with stout, erect stems, bell-shaped red, orange or yellow flowers and has got a strong scent similar to garlic. In its home region Anatolia and Central Asia, it grows on rocks, in rocky slopes and among ahurbs at an altitude of 1000-3000 meters. As a spring bulb it combines well with eg Tulips, Daffodils and Grape Hyacinths.
The cultivar 'Lutea' has been documented 1809 at the latest. It has got a bright yellow flower with slightly green flower veins and this cultivar grows somewhat shorter than the species, 80-100 cm high.
